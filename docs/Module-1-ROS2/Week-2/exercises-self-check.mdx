# Exercises and Self-Check Questions - Week 2: Sensors & Landscape

## Chapter 1: Sensor Overview (LiDAR, Camera, IMU, Force/Torque)

### Conceptual Questions
1. Compare the working principles of LiDAR, cameras, IMUs, and force/torque sensors. What physical phenomena does each sensor measure?
2. Explain the concept of sensor fusion and why it's important in humanoid robotics.
3. What are the main limitations of each sensor type, and how can these be addressed through sensor fusion?

### Analysis Questions
4. For indoor navigation, compare the effectiveness of LiDAR, cameras, and IMUs. When would you use each sensor or combination of sensors?
5. Analyze the trade-offs between different sensor types in terms of cost, accuracy, power consumption, and environmental robustness.
6. Explain how drift in IMU measurements can be corrected using other sensor types.

### Application Questions
7. Design a sensor configuration for a humanoid robot intended for warehouse navigation. Justify your choices considering the environment and tasks.
8. For a robot performing delicate manipulation tasks, which sensors are most critical? Explain how they work together.
9. Investigate a recent advancement in one of the sensor types. How does it address current limitations?

## Chapter 2: Humanoid Robotics Landscape

### Knowledge Questions
1. Identify three major commercial humanoid robot platforms and compare their target applications and capabilities.
2. Describe the key features that differentiate research robots from commercial robots.
3. Name three regional centers of humanoid robotics development and their particular focus areas.

### Analysis Questions
4. Compare the design philosophies of different humanoid robots: biomimetic vs. functional vs. hybrid approaches. What are the trade-offs of each?
5. Analyze the reasons behind the discontinuation of Honda's ASIMO. What does this tell us about the commercial viability of humanoid robots?
6. Evaluate the factors that influence the success of a humanoid robot platform in the market.

### Design Questions
7. Design a humanoid robot for a specific application (e.g., elderly care, education, industry). Justify your design choices regarding size, sensors, actuators, and capabilities.
8. Research a humanoid robot not mentioned in the chapter. Describe its unique capabilities and applications.
9. What factors would you consider when choosing between different humanoid robot platforms for a research project?

## Chapter 3: Interaction with Human Environments

### Scenario-Based Questions
1. Design safety protocols for a humanoid robot operating in a children's hospital. Consider physical safety, psychological safety, and emergency procedures.
2. How would you modify a humanoid robot to safely navigate a busy airport environment? Address navigation, safety, and interaction challenges.
3. Create interaction protocols for a service robot in a restaurant setting. Consider different user types and scenarios.

### Analysis Questions
4. Analyze the challenges of designing robots for elderly care facilities. What specific considerations must be addressed?
5. Explain how social conventions (personal space, right of way, social timing) affect robot navigation in human environments.
6. Discuss the ethical implications of humanoid robots in human environments. What guidelines should govern their deployment?

### Technical Questions
7. How would you implement human-aware navigation for a robot moving through crowded spaces? What systems would be required?
8. Design a multi-modal communication system for human-robot interaction in public spaces.
9. Explain how robots can adapt to individual users' preferences and needs in shared environments.

## Synthesis Questions (Cross-Chapter)

1. How do the sensor capabilities of a humanoid robot affect its ability to interact safely and effectively in human environments?
2. Design a sensor suite for a humanoid robot intended for home care assistance. Consider the challenges from Chapter 3 and the sensor types from Chapter 1.
3. How do different humanoid robot platforms (Chapter 2) approach the challenges of human environment interaction (Chapter 3)?
4. Analyze how sensor limitations might restrict the applications of humanoid robots in human environments.
